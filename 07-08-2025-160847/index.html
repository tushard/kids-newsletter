Why Do We Have Hair on Our Heads?

Did you ever wonder why people have fur only on their heads and not all over their bodies like many animals? Scientists think it helped our early human ancestors stay cool in the hot sun. Strands of hair made a little air cushion that stopped too much heat from reaching their skin.

Over time, these ancestors stood up tall and walked on two feet, which meant their heads got extra sunshine. Having a head full of hair, especially curly or dense hair, was like wearing a natural sunhat. It also protected their developing brains. As these humans moved into different parts of the world, their hair came in many colors and textures, from straight to wavy, to suit new climates.
